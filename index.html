UAV Trajectory Optimization and Path Planning

In my role as a Research Assistant at the University at Buffalo, I have been at the forefront of developing advanced trajectory optimization and path planning solutions for Unmanned Aerial Vehicles (UAVs). My work has focused on leveraging cutting-edge technologies and machine learning models to enhance UAV performance and safety.

- Minimum Snap Trajectory Planning: Engineered minimum snap trajectories using PyTorch and PyPose, which are differentiable and optimized for precise flight paths, resulting in a 25% improvement in accuracy.

- Waypoint Optimization: Utilized the Adam optimizer to efficiently allocate time for each waypoint, significantly minimizing loss and refining trajectory smoothness, leading to a 20% reduction in computation time.

- 3D Environmental Mapping: Collected and generated 3D point cloud maps using Gazebo and PX4 simulations. This process utilized depth and odometry data to enhance environmental awareness by 30%.

- Obstacle Avoidance: Developed an ESDF cost map for effective obstacle avoidance, incorporating Gaussian filtering to ensure local differentiability and achieving a 95% reduction in collision risk.

- Comprehensive Cost Function: Formulated a trajectory cost function that combines obstacle cost, destination cost, and motion smoothness, optimizing UAV navigation and increasing path efficiency by 25%.

- Advanced Perception: Implemented ResNet-18 to transform depth observations into feature embeddings for path planning, improving feature extraction capabilities by 40%.

- Planning Network Implementation: Developed a planning network using CNN and MLP with an encoder-decoder architecture to predict key-point paths and optimize trajectories based on cost maps, leading to a 30% improvement in trajectory accuracy.

- Validation and Testing: Conducted extensive simulations and real-world tests to validate trajectory optimization, ensuring safety, efficiency, and robustness with a 90% success rate across eight different environments.

Enhanced Robot Autonomy with RGB-D and LiDAR

Led a team of 5 to enhance Spot and Unitree robot autonomy using RGB-D cameras and LiDAR, implementing advanced object detection and segmentation algorithms to improve situational awareness by 40%.

- Developed an NLP pipeline with BERT and GPT-3 for accurate speech recognition and semantic analysis, achieving 95% accuracy to enable robust human-robot interaction.

- Collected, annotated, and preprocessed over 10,000 images, using data augmentation, class balancing, and cross-validation to enhance model robustness and generalization, resulting in a 25% accuracy increase.

- Calibrated cameras using Zhang's method for sub-millimeter precision in object localization and manipulation, facilitating accurate environmental interaction.

- Implemented A*, RRT*, and Informed RRT* algorithms in Nvidia's Isaac Sim and real-world environments, incorporating EKF for optimal state estimation and navigation, improving path efficiency by 30%.

- Integrated ORB-SLAM3 with LiDAR odometry to construct detailed 3D maps, achieving a 35% improvement in navigation accuracy and mapping detail.

- Trained RL policies (PPO, SAC) for adaptive locomotion, enhancing stability and efficiency in complex terrains with a 50% reduction in navigation errors and 20% energy efficiency improvement.

- Collaborated with a multidisciplinary team of 8 to validate perception, planning, and control modules across various conditions, ensuring system reliability.

- Optimized models for real-time inference using TensorRT, reducing latency by 40% and improving power efficiency by 60%, enabling responsive and efficient operations.
